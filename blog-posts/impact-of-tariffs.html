The Impact of Tariffs on Series As

April 7, 2025

The recent implementation of tariffs has sent ripples through the startup ecosystem, particularly affecting companies in the midst of Series A funding rounds. As someone who has guided multiple startups through critical growth phases, I've observed firsthand how these economic policy shifts can dramatically alter the landscape for emerging companies.

Series A represents a pivotal moment for startups—it's when they've proven their concept and are ready to scale. However, the introduction of tariffs creates several layers of complexity that founders must now navigate carefully.

Direct Cost Implications

For hardware startups or those with physical products in their supply chain, the impact is immediate and tangible. Manufacturing costs increase, margins shrink, and pricing strategies require recalibration. This shift in unit economics can fundamentally alter the growth projections that form the backbone of Series A pitch decks.

One founder I recently coached saw their cost of goods sold increase by 23% virtually overnight. This required not only revising their financial models but rethinking their entire go-to-market strategy to maintain competitive positioning while preserving sustainable margins.

Investor Sentiment Shifts

Beyond the direct financial impact, tariffs have created a noticeable shift in investor psychology. VCs are increasingly factoring macroeconomic uncertainty into their decision-making process, often resulting in:

- More conservative valuations
- Higher expectations for runway efficiency
- Greater emphasis on domestic market traction
- Increased scrutiny of supply chain resilience

This doesn't mean Series A funding has dried up—rather, the bar has been raised, and founders must be exceptionally prepared to address these new concerns proactively.

Strategic Adaptations

The most successful founders I've worked with are treating this challenge as an opportunity to demonstrate their adaptability—a quality investors prize highly. Some effective strategies include:

Supply Chain Diversification

Companies that previously relied on single-source suppliers are now developing multi-region manufacturing capabilities. This not only mitigates tariff impacts but creates operational resilience that strengthens their overall business model.

Accelerated Software Components

Hardware-focused startups are increasingly emphasizing the software and services aspects of their offerings, which are less affected by tariffs. This shift often reveals new revenue streams that actually improve their long-term business outlook.

Strategic Fundraising Timing

Some founders are adjusting their fundraising timelines—either accelerating to secure capital before further economic shifts or delaying to demonstrate their ability to execute under the new conditions.

Looking Forward

While tariffs create undeniable challenges for startups approaching Series A, they also serve as a forcing function for building more resilient businesses. The companies that successfully adapt will emerge stronger, with business models better equipped to weather future economic fluctuations.

For founders currently navigating this landscape, I recommend focusing on transparent communication with both investors and customers. Clearly articulate how you're addressing these challenges and transforming them into strategic advantages. This demonstrates the leadership and adaptability that remain the true north star for Series A investors, regardless of economic conditions.

The path to Series A has never been easy—and while tariffs add new obstacles, they haven't fundamentally changed what makes startups successful: solving real problems with innovative approaches and demonstrating the leadership required to execute through changing conditions.
